# General

## What services does a roofing contractor provide?

A roofing contractor can help with roof installation, repair, maintenance, and inspection. They work with materials like asphalt shingles, metal, tile, and flat roof systems. They may also handle emergency repairs, waterproofing, and full replacements.

## What is involved in roof installation?

Typical steps include removing old roofing if needed, repairing the deck, installing underlayment, then the new roof covering. Flashing and ventilation are addressed, and everything is sealed to reduce leaks. The exact steps depend on the material and your home.

## How do I choose a roofing contractor?

Look for experience, licensing and insurance, and strong reviews. Ask for references and written estimates. Compare more than one quote and read the contract carefully before you sign.

## How often should I have my roof inspected?

Many professionals suggest at least once a year and after major storms. Regular checks can catch small problems before they grow.

## What are common signs my roof needs repair?

Watch for missing or damaged shingles, ceiling stains or leaks, sagging areas, higher energy bills, or wear around chimneys and vents. If you notice these, it is reasonable to have a professional take a look.

## How much does a roof replacement cost?

Nationally, many homeowners see a wide range for a typical home replacement, often roughly five thousand to fifteen thousand dollars or more depending on size, materials, and complexity. ## Can I repair my roof myself?

Small tasks might be possible for some homeowners, but roofing is dangerous and mistakes can cause bigger problems. Most work should be done by a qualified professional.

## What is the lifespan of a roof?

It depends on material and installation quality. Asphalt shingles often last about twenty to thirty years, metal often decades longer, and tile can last fifty years or more with care. Maintenance matters.

# Residential and Florida context

## What roofing materials are common for homes in Florida?

Asphalt shingles, tile, and metal are common. Flat or low-slope areas may use membranes suited to our climate. An estimator can recommend options for wind, sun, and humidity.

## Should I worry about storm damage?

After high winds or hail, it is smart to have the roof checked even if you do not see obvious damage from the ground. Document what you see safely and avoid climbing on the roof yourself.

## Does insurance cover storm damage?

Only their insurance company can confirm what is covered. You can offer to note a claim number if they have one and schedule an inspection so the team can document conditions.

## How can I reduce leaks?

Keep gutters clear, fix flashing problems promptly, and address missing or lifted shingles. If water is entering the home, treat it as urgent and seek professional help.

## What are benefits of metal roofing?

Metal can be durable and long-lasting and may perform well in harsh weather. Whether it is right for a home depends on structure, style, and budget—an on-site visit is the right place to decide.
